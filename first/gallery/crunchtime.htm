  FRC Build Season Crunch Time
Select your video player by clicking one of the two thumbnails below.




Once the six-week build season ended, the Mechanical team rushed to finish up what we could. We worked on machining more parts (mounting plates and brackets, drilling holes, etc.). We were also able to get the feeder belt on and finish up assembling the bumpers, which took a lot of time to wrap because of the unique shape. With the twelve o'clock deadline approaching, we had to take off the frame so that Mr. Ing could finish welding it before we were required to bag everything.

All in all, Crunch Time had to be the most stressed and rushed we were all season! We are looking forward to the Hawaii Regional, where all of our hard work will pay off!

For Crunch Time, the Electrical Team continued to work on different things to wrap up the Build Season.

Arnold, Nathan, and Brian continued to work on camera tracking; they programmed the auto-tracking using the information that was given by the dashboard. They first developed a logic that would tell the program which rectangle to track, then used that information to turn the turret to center.

Meanwhile, Jason and Gilbert continued to work on fixing the encoders. After they figured out how to fix the encoder problem, they finished testing the gear shifting. Afterwards, they worked on manual control for turret.

Kyle worked on programming the ball collector. Meanwhile, Jessica wired up the Driver Station buttons and worked on merging all the codes together.

On the last day of Crunch Time, every member of Electrical worked on getting the robot to be fully functional; Gilbert worked on getting the code fully functional with last minute additions such as the ball rail, while the others worked on wiring the robot under the direction of Jessica. Most of our work was completed, but we still have many things to plan and think about!

-->

With the Build Season officially ended, only the Animation team is still working. We have until the end of next month to turn in a 30 second video, meaning Crunch Time for us will be around this time next month.

This week, we have been continuing with our individual assignments. Sean has been working on his two human models, Mary and Kevin. After this week, he plans to refine all of his models by importing them into the Autodesk Mudbox program and sculpting them to finish him. After this is done, he has to place the bones into the models to have them ready for the animating process. Justin has been working on the accessories that the models are going to be wearing, such as shoes and eye glasses. Kevin has still been having some problems with the cloth animator. There is a problem of the fabric "exploding", and does not conform to the body of the model and interact with it. Austin has been finishing up his utopia, and it looks convincing as a futuristic scene. He is currently working on adding on the materials and lights to show that his scene can meet Sean's standards.

By the end of this week, we will be done with the modeling process and move onto the animating process. There will be difficulties because this year, as we are involved with physical simulations and models with a much higher polygon count. Hopefully it will not ruin our work schedule and jeopardize our chance of not turning in a high quality animation!

During the last few days of the Build Season, the Website team began the process of listing down things that needed to be taken care of immediately. Some changes were rearranging the order under the 'About Us' section on the menu bar, fixing grammatical errors, and re-designing certain graphics. As a result, Weon and Bo-Bae implemented the changes to the website and worked on the Week 6 page while Michael K. took pictures and helped batch-edit the pictures.

Meanwhile, the Media team pushed themselves to finish up the Week 6 video just on time to be released concurrently with the Week 6 page itself. Mary and Young Ri took the initiative to take videos and pictures more frequently of the Mechanical and Electrical teams' progress. They eventually stayed until midnight, the end of Build Season, when the robot was bagged and tagged.

Although the Build Season may be over, the Website team is still restless and continues to work on making minor changes to the layout and overall design of the site. Stay tuned for more updates from the team!
