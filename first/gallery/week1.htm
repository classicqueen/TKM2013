  FRC Build Season Week 1
Select your video player by clicking one of the two thumbnails below.




The first week of the build season started off with a TV stand being made for the school as practice with our new welder. This week also involved quite a bit of brain-storming and thinking up strategies, like the best drive and scoring methods. Also, our wood shop teacher was able to make the barrier for us so we were able to test out the kit chassis. It did not go over too smoothly, so another test chassis was constructed using small wheels in the front. Towards the end of the week, we were able to get some machining done for a mount for the CNC. Hopefully, we will get to prototype more.

This week, we started off with the Kickoff event, introducing this year's game of 'Rebound Rumble'. We separated out the parts in the Kit of Parts (KOP) and tested the various electrical components using the bench top test. We tested both the electronics and pneumatics and found a few flawed parts such as the DB 37 cable and the digital sidecar. We solved several problems concerning our code for the bench top testing, one being the compressor's start up.

After completing the bench top testing, we moved on to learning how to splice wires from Jessica. Splicing involves heating the wires using the soldering iron and touching the connected wires with the solder in order for the heat to transfer to the solder via the bound wires. In the midst of our training this week, we had a visitation from mentors from rookie Team 4270 - Saint Louis School. We demonstrated to them the projects we were working on and aided them to the best of our abilities by explaining to them what we were doing in preparation for the official building.

The last part of our experience for this week was the start up of the camera for the Kinect; set-up and reconfiguration was necessary, taking up quite a bit of time. However, we are still far from finishing the calibration of the camera exposure and settings which we intend to work on in Week Two. We also worked on constructing the backboard for the game by using a cardboard box and the appropriate tape to replicate it accurately. We hope to be able to get more things done in Week Two!

The first week for the Animation team was a very interesting experience. On the 12th, our theme for the Animation competition came out; the topic dealt with the Iiko's coming to help us. The Iiko's come to planet Earth to help our education system with either an idea, product, or invention. This topic is very frustrating. When we first brain-stormed, our team came up with the idea that the Iiko's would help our education system by motivating students and showing them all the technology that they, the Iiko's, have created on their home planet.

Also during this week, Sasaki, our mentor, came in and offered some constructive criticism. We're now coming up with more ideas to express our views on the theme. As for our works in progress, Justin has been working on making a classroom since our take on the theme so far has been focusing on a classroom setting. Sean, on the other hand, has been working on the face of characters that will be in the animation, and has been working on the space ship that the Iiko's will arrive in. Meanwhile, Kevin has been working continually on creating 3D models of everyday objects to place into the classroom.

In conclusion, Animation has been making steady progress and expects to get quite a bit done by next week!

This week, both parts of the Website team got many things done. The actual Website team worked on the 2012 VEX site (Bo-Bae), fixed the 2012 FRC Site for bugs and glitches (Weon), and started on making graphics to feature on the sites (Michael K.).

The Media team worked on compiling a video report for Week 1 of the Build Season, complete with voice-over and footage from working teams (Young-Ri), and taking additional photos and video for more things to place on the website (Mary L.).

The Website team worked hard all week and got fantastic results that bring us much closer to our goal of a website with interesting and relevant pictures and easier navigation for users. We hope to complete the VEX and FIRST sites and upload our finished product very soon; meanwhile, we will be uploading weekly video reports of the Build Season and posting pictures and starting daily Twitter feeds!
