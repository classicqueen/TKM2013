  FRC Build Season Week 5
Select your video player by clicking one of the two thumbnails below.




The Mechanical Team spent Week Five working on the shooter. We had to assemble it with new wheels, then make shafts for it so that it could have an encoder. Spacers and various plates were made. There was also a lot of testing done, shooting from various distances and angles in order to see what works best and then taking down measurements. Mr. Ing obtained a new cable spool so that we could use it for the ball feeder. Towards the end of the week, we had to take apart our chassis to cut the ends to help it go over the bump.

During this week we concentrated our efforts to get components of our final code to be assembled together. Some of us had worked on the camera centering while others worked to program other parts of the robot such as gear shifting. As of this point, we had split into various sub groups to work on various things to increase efficiency. Although we were not successful in all our tasks, we were able to complete a portion of the tasks we were given such as working with the X value of the tracking code and the concept of "sticky keys".

Modeling is progressing at a slow but sure rate. With a little less than two months left, we have quite a few models in our storage and it seems that we are making good time.

Austin has made some progress with his Utopia and his new concepts are very interesting. His structure formation is incredibly creative. He is progressing well and has also started delving into the world of hair. Although somewhat skeptical of the idea, Austin looks forward to what he will create such as a mohawk and afro hair style which would be very interesting to view.

Kevin has also progressed greatly in the time that has passed this week with just recently completing his very impressive and detailed model of A-Building. Currently working with cloth materials that will cover the character models, he faces obstacles and frustrations but otherwise he shows great talent and potential. Learning the tools such as garment maker and cloth materials themselves is a long and arduous process but Kevin pushes on and has made some accomplishments.

As for Justin, human modeling continues to be a struggle. The specific details of the face are very confusing as when one polygon is extruded, the next cannot be connected correctly or in one view it seems fine but with the turbo-smooth filter run, it turns out strangely.

Sean continues to make great progress completing multiple models such as more character models, an auditorium setting to be used in one of the scenes and is nearing completion of a female character model.

We are planning a meeting in order to redefine our goals and make sure everyone is clear on what they should be doing and creating a set of deadlines to add organization to the group.

Now that time is dwindling down, Website has been working diligently to fix up the new site to be re-released to the public. Some of the work we've done is gear the site to be more user-friendly and promote our social media pages (Twitter, YouTube, Facebook). We have added more CSS codes and created more graphics in Photoshop.

Media had quite a bit of footage this week, beginning with a demo that the team did (headed by captains Bo-Bae, Jessica, and Weon), pictures of Mechanical putting together the robot, and ending with interviews from a member of all of the sub-teams.

From this point forward, we hope to update our page even more consistently than before, so that curious users can track our progress even closer than before and be notified of events (more demonstrations)! Check out all the pages of our new site to see what has changed!
