  FRC Build Season Week 2
Select your video player by clicking one of the two thumbnails below.




For the mechanical team, Week 2 was spent making some of the robot parts. We made sprockets, drive shafts, bearing blocks, and hubs. The sprockets needed to be cut down and broached on the lathe and then finished up on the CNC mill, where Mr. Ing showed us how to use CAM to create the program to cut the sprocket. Also the drive shafts was cut down to precision on the lathe and finished up on the CNC mill. We also assembled the bearing blocks by pushing the dowel pins into it using the vice.

Along with that, we discussed how we wanted the drive train to be and also how we should grab and score the balls. Stay tuned next week for more progress!

This week started off with the mounting of our benchtop board onto a chassis. The idea was to get the components in place and be able to drive the chassis around using an arcade drive. Much of our time had been dedicated to troubleshooting a strange pulsating pattern in the drive; various solutions arose, some pertaining to the speed of the project, the polymorphic speed controller selector virtual instrument, and the camera code which had been implemented the week before. We overcame this obstacle by simply going through each solution step by step and eventually starting from scratch on a clean project.

We also learned about editing the dashboard on LabVIEW, a session taught by Jason himself. Kyle had continued his progression into the C-programming language to help with the co-processor, and spent his time observing its progress while the rest of Electrical spent our time testing replaced parts such as the DB-37 and the digital sidecar. Our week ended with the introduction to the Cypress board, being able to note the various ports and attaching on a pair of switches to display values on the dashboard.

Jessica and Gilbert dedicated their time mainly to overseeing our progress as well as towards the completion of the co-processor, with the aid of mentors. Their working time extended over the course of two days, working on the coprocessor while troubleshooting with a practice robot.

Over the past week, we learned many skills regarding troubleshooting problems in code and the conventions for wiring speed controllers onto motors. Many of us found this week to be difficult, in the sense that we felt lost when troubleshooting due to the many difficulties we found with reading the various virtual instruments, which are foreign to us; we plan to gain more practice by dissecting codes piece by piece in order to prevent large changes that cannot be fixed.

This week we made quite a lot of progress in terms of modeling. Austin created an assortment of objects that will be used in the classroom scene and has also started work on a futuristic city that might be used. Kevin has been continuing his work on a model of the Administration Building while Sean and Justin have been working on original characters.

While there is no final script for the animation, the team hopes to create a storyline that will be plausible and believable. Overall, we are making a lot of progress in terms of our work with modeling. A lot of models have been made and we are making smooth progress. There is still a lot of work ahead of us though and it won't be easy, but we'll make it!

This week marked the official public release of our 2012 FIRST team website, with content that covered the first week of Build Season (weekly reports, pictures, and weekly videos).

Michael K. began to create graphics for the website (the icons on the 'Build Season' page, and the content slider on the homepage). Weon and Bo-Bae continued to troubleshoot problems brought up by other members, mentors and advisers. They updated the images for the 2011 page in 'Past Competitions', the information in 'Resources', and the images in 'Mentors'. The Media team (Mary and Young-Ri) cranked out the Week 1 and Kickoff videos, but faced technical problems when exporting the videos as their files were much too big. Luckily, they fixed the problem by adjusting their encoding setting, and have just finished filming content for the Week 2 video. They are currently in the process of editing the Week 2 video and having it posted as soon it is complete. Stay tuned!
