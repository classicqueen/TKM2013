  FRC Build Season Week 4
Select your video player by clicking one of the two thumbnails below.




Week Four was a busy week for Mechanical with the deadline approaching. We finished putting together our chassis and BAD (Ball Acquisition Device) and started on our shooter. We ran in to a few problems with the chassis after welding it together. It bent a little so we had to bend it back where it then cracked and had to be re-welded. The BAD is capable of collecting the balls rather effectively, but now we have to figure out how to keep it from only possessing three at a time. This week new members were able to learn how to broach key-ways. We ended Week Four by making parts for the shooter.

This week started off with problems concerning robot communication; many of the codes we attempted to import onto the robot as start-up were not able to be successfully loaded. Many of us were confused as to what the problem was, but we discovered later that the power wires connecting the cRIO and power distribution board were to blame.

Gilbert and Jessica worked to help the rest of the team complete our tasks and also took the time to test various electrical components such as the Cypress board and the sensors. Kyle had worked on his C-programming training and worked on various tasks he was assigned to. Jason worked on the 'Elapsed Times.vi' to calculate the time it takes for one iteration for each VI. Brian, Arnold and Nathan worked on various tasks from testing out the speed controllers and sampling autonomous code to the camera dashboard configuration. A majority of our time was devoted to troubleshooting connection problems with the practice robot this week, the problem occurring off and on.

Animation would be a boring thing to do if you could not find the drive to create models composed of thousands of polygons. Without the motivation it is very hard to spend many hours in front of the computer manipulating some polygons. This idea resonates throughout our animation topic on how motivation is the key to succeed, and without it it is hard to produce good things even if you have the materialistic goods to produce great things. I hope that everyone still has that motivation and is seeking the inspiration to create an exceptional animation for this year.

Many of us have been continuing our work from last week. Sean produced more human characters, Kevin finished up the A-Building model, Austin worked on the utopia, and Justin familiarized himself with modeling humans. Kevin's model was surprisingly realistic, while Austin was slightly deviating away from what Sean planned, though he understood what Sean wanted and that it was important to give 100% for each model. Next week, we hope some to get into the next step of modeling other objects. Kevin has already started doing research on cloths to cover the human models, since he is the 'specialist' on this topic. Overall, we believe we are advancing towards the greatest animation ever created in our team and it is vital to keep it that way.

The Media team has continued to take pictures for documentation, and filmed many of our subgroup's progress in hopes of utilizing them in our Week 4 video of the Build Season. They have also interviewed a person from every subgroup in finding out what is their current status on their work, and got a different person to proceed on providing the voice-overs for the video. Afterwards, the Media team has got down to editing and is in the final development phase of rearranging videos, while also adding subtitles and background music.

This week was dedicated to finishing up the site; thankfully, it is nearing completion. The Website team has been working hard redesigning the website, ewriting many CSS properties to get the results we wanted and redesigning the graphics for faster loading time. On top of that, we decided to implement a new menu bar for our website to approach a more streamlined website. However, we encountered many problems since the majority of its codes were located in JavaScript, which left the team staring at foreign codes and working on the wrong files. With the generous help and knowledge from our mentor John, we were able to get the new menu bar working and are currently tweaking that up along with the side banners we're planning to describe each corresponding FIRST Subgroups. The website shall soon go live!
