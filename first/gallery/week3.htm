  FRC Build Season Week 3
Select your video player by clicking one of the two thumbnails below.




During the third week of the Build Season, the Mechanical team worked on making more parts for the robot such as the pins used to tension the chain, the transmission mount, the chassis frame, hubs, spacers, and tubing holders. We also tested the wheel configuration more and finally decided on a drive for our robot. We tested out the shooter with an old robot to see how well it would shoot the balls and found our idea was pretty decent. With the help of the McKinley Construction Academy students, we were able to get the goals built and assembled. After finishing up the last two sprockets on the CNC, we're aiming to get the chains on in Week Four.

At the start of this week, the first year members got the practice chassis to drive properly after numerous attempts and hours of troubleshooting. We also received a new router and set it up to use with the practice chassis. Later in the week we also received a new joystick, which we mapped out and reconfigured in order to make our drive compatible with it. Weon tried the drive with the new joystick and seemed to be satisfied with the control. Arnold and Brian tested the new Cypress and put it on the casing while Gilbert analyzed different C codes from the 2008 season to see how we could get the sensors working on our co-processor. Jessica went from group to group, assisting with any trouble they were experiencing and Jason was working on the FMS lite, a field management program.

Throughout the week Brian built practice goals with pieces of plywood. Overall, the third week of the Build Season was filled with many frustrating moments caused by many problems that we encountered. However, we did achieve quite a few things this week, and we got to review several key concepts from the prior weeks. We gained a lot of knowledge from all the problems we experienced this week and were able to enjoy the fruits of our labor.

Over the course of this week, we have been making a steady progress. The general storyboard has been decided with only the dialogue to go. The models are coming along pretty well with a high poly count for each of them.

Sean has been working on fine-tuning two human models. It was a challenge working on these two models because they are organic objects and it becomes quite evident that something is not right when looking into their faces since we are so used to looking at each other's faces. He was also working on the spaceship, which became another challenge due to there not being any real spaceships to use as reference. Justin has been working with the human models also. He enjoys creating them but, understandably, has some trouble at the same time working with them.

Kevin has been working on the school's Administration building. We thought it was aesthetically pleasing and decided to incorporate it into the animation. So far so good until he started to work on the pillars of the structure. Kevin was given a challenge to finish up those pillars by researching a method to complete them. Austin has been working on the utopia, which is a physical representation of the future which we strive for. We were very amazed at the concept of the different structures; he has been working with many reference pictures from the Web, and incorporated several concepts to create an incredibly unique design. Hopefully, he will be able to continue this process and not run out of ideas in creating these structures.

For next week, we hope to finish our current tasks and start doing research on different fields of specialization. Such fields include clothing, hair, rigging, and texturing. By assigning each of the team members to work on a different topic, our efficiency and the quality of our work will increase.

This week was filled with trials and hurdles for the Website team to conquer. The beginning of the week started out normally, with pictures and video being taken and edited to go on our site for the end of Week 3. However, several problems with the site layout occurred and a change was necessary to fix it, so the team is currently revamping the whole site to provide a newer, better version of the previous 2012 site to present to the public by the middle of next week.
