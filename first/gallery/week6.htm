  FRC Build Season Week 6
Select your video player by clicking one of the two thumbnails below.




During Week Six, the Mechanical Team worked on making some parts for the shooter and adjustments to the chassis; we also began working on the bumpers. For the chassis, we made several spacers and plugs. Mr. Ing did some more welding on the chassis, attaching the bumper and BAD (Ball Acquisition Device) mounts. As for the bumpers, we made the mounts for it and cut out the fabric. We ended the week by testing the shooter's angle and length to help finalize a design.

This week, the Electrical Team started off with prioritizing; we made a list of things to do before the Bag and Tag date. Afterwards, we broke up into groups to start working on different projects.

Arnold, Brian, and Nathan continued to work with the camera to get the auto-tracking and centering working. Throughout the week, they changed the dashboard to display the processed image. Meanwhile, Gilbert and Jason worked on the gear shifting; while working on the shifting process, they discovered that this year's encoder VI's still had the same bug as last years'. They fixed the bug by stringing the real encoder VI and the 'dummy' encoder VI together.

Kyle worked on getting the line tracker to detect the Key by running the simple DIO example, but could not get it to read the tracker correctly. Jessica helped to troubleshoot and discovered that it was actually the example code was incorrect. It was using a shift register to feed the DIO VI every loop with its device reference, which was the reason why the code was not working. Jessica continued to tweak on the flywheel to increase the consistency. The RPM of the flywheel stayed within 30 RPM of the desired RPM, resulting in 80% consistency.

All of the Animation team made progress this week and we are slowly getting closer to having all of our model elements ready to be put together and animated. With a month and a half left before the deadline, we are making good progress and trying to keep one week open before the due date for final revisions.

Austin has been continuing his Utopia; he has an idea to incorporate "Easter eggs" into his structures and is in the beginning phases of researching how to model hair. Kevin continues to work with creating cloth that will be applied to the character models. By exploring more into the process of cloth modeling and learning through trial and error, Kevin's work is a crucial part of keeping our animation Robotics and family-friendly. Justin is finishing up a model of shoes and working on the classroom setting by incorporating components such as windows as doors. Sean is finishing up his latest character model that will be added to our collection. He is also starting on his next character, which will be a female model and is expected to finish within two days.

With so many components done or being worked on, our animation could be finished sooner and more smoothly than we had initially hoped!

Website's week was a scramble for the right timing to grab the right shots; Weon and mentor Mr. Paik worked on uploading a new page ("About the Site") to the website while Bo-Bae re-wrote some more information to be put on the page. Michael K. learned about some more settings on his camera as well as more techniques to getting his pictures to have more impact. Meanwhile, Young Ri and Mary L. of the Media team managed to create and upload their Week 5 video at the beginning of the week and are working on doing the same with the Week 6 video. Website also took the time to make spirit items for the two regionals our team is planning on entering, creating graphics to cut out, laminate, and attach to the items.
