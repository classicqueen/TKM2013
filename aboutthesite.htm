// Introducing our 2012 FIRST Robotics Team Website

// What else is new?

// Cross-Browser Compatible

The 2012 FIRST Robotics Team Website is compatible with many of today's browsers; such browsers include Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox 2.0+, Internet Explorer 7+, and Safari. To conform to most Web standards, our site does not support Internet Explorer 6 and below. A warning was created to detect users who peruse the site with an outdated browser or a browser that has disabled JavaScript.


// New Menubar

We implemented a new menu bar design intended to be more pleasing to the eye; the new menu bar is capable of producing a fluid, streamlined animation whenever the dropdown is accessed, as well as an indicator to inform the user which category a page falls under.


// Google Calendar

The Google Calendar located on the front page of our website has been implemented as a way of allowing users to keep track of our progress outside of the Build Season. Some of the events that can be seen are team meetings, public demonstrations, crucial deadlines for FIRST, and dates for competitions we will be entering. Using the calendar, people can see what we do around our community and how our team manages its time.


// Technically Diverse

To the Website team, being technically diverse means being able to create an easier user interface from different website languages. For the purpose of achieving an aesthetically pleasing, clean website without having to experience a long loading period, we have implemented HTML, CSS, and JavaScript codes.

// Driven by Students
Students who join the Website team learn, at minimum, the basics of several website languages ranging from HTML to CSS and even JavaScript. The team then applies their knowledge of codes to write their own designs or manipulate existing ones to add on to the site. Student mentors also take the time to explain each program that is used throughout the season, such as Adobe Dreamweaver CS5 and Adobe Photoshop CS5. Each section of this website was carefully designed in such a way so as to maximize ease of accessibility and user convenience. The current website is the sixth major version of the team website since the team's founding in 1999.

// Socially Connected
Through the use of popular social networks (Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, etc.), Team 368 can update the public on the team's progress frequently using sites that most people already have access to. This also helps team announcements and important information to be passed more quickly and reliably than through e-mail or phone. The team also utilizes Google Sites, with a page dedicated to each sub-team (Electrical, Mechanical, Website, and Animation) to gather daily and weekly reports to be compiled and added to the main site in order to allow users to track our progress.

// Community Impact
The McKinley Robotics Team website was designed with the intention of creating an easier browsing experience for users to view our progress and also to inform more people about the FIRST Robotics program. Our website can be accessed by anyone and everyone, including people from around the world: on average (according to Google Analytics), our site is visited nearly two thousand times a month and by people from our local community, the nation, and from all over the world (e.g.: China, Korea, France).
