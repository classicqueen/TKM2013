Logo Motion

In Logo Motion two alliances, composed of three robots each, compete on a flat 27' x 54' foot field. Each alliance attempts to score as many points as possible on their half of the field by hanging as many inflated plastic triangles, squares, and circles as they can in a two minute and fifteen second period. There are also Ubertubes included with the game objects, and the higher up the inflatables are hung, the more points they are worth for each alliance.

The match is split up into two parts: the first is a fifteen second 'Autonomous' period in which there is no driver control and the robots must independently try to score objects for extra points. The second part is the interactive portion, where drivers control the robot and try to score as many pieces as possible. Any game piece that is hung on the same peg as an Ubertube is worth double points, and if an alliance manages to hang the game pieces horizontally in the same formation as the FIRST logo (triangle, circle, square), then all of the points for that row are doubled.

With time dwindling down, the match is concluded with robots deploying minibots, which are smaller robots that operate separately from the main competing robot; the minibots must climb to the top of a vertical pole to trigger a sensor and earn bonus points, and thus ends the game.

The Robot

Name: Paik Man

Abilities:
- 6 wheel drive with high traction treads
- 2 speed automatic transmission driven by 4 CIMs
- Wide gripper - powered by pneumatics
- 3 motor elevator lift (with programmed set heights)
- Contains a minibot (climb + deploy time: 1.233 sec)
