Besides participating in competitions, the McKinley Robotics Team also holds demonstrations for various public events. At these events, we showcase our robots from programs we have previously competed in. By doing this, we generate interest among the audience and get them involved in what we do. Members from our team also interact with the audience and answer their questions, sharing their enthusiasm for robotics. Some of our demonstrations include:

2011 Hawaii ROC Pau Hana

On July 28th, 2011, the Robotics Organizing Committee (ROC) organized a Pau Hana ("finished work" in Hawaiian) event. The event was held to officially conclude the 2010-2011 Robotics season and also as preparation for starting the 2011-2012 season! The team was invited to this event to speak to company representatives, officials, and even the former governor of Hawaii, Linda Lingle. We also collaborated with other Hawaii teams to model a new clothing line released by ROC as a means of fundraising.

The McKinley Robotics Team showcased their 2010 VEX robot at Wet 'N Wild Hawaii on July 22nd, 2011. The demonstration featured students from the Kapiolani Community College (KCC) STEM Summer Bridge Program, helping to discover common interests among the viewers and showing what Hawaii's students are capable of through Robotics.

2011 FIRST Lego League Volunteering

Jessica, Kyle, and Arnold of TKM made visits every Tuesday and Thursday or the VEX season to assist and mentor Ka'ahumanu Elementary School's FIRST Lego League team. There, the FLL team was assisted by the Robotics members who gave constructive criticism and shared their opinions on project layouts and designs, but also helped to troubleshoot with problems in the FLL team's program.

2011 O'ahu In STEP Event

At the Hawaii Convention Center on September 1st, 2011, Team Kika Mana showcased their 2011 FRC robot as well as their 2011 VEX Gateway robots to students in elementary and middle school. The demo showed off the drivers and controllers, as well as introduced the younger level students to the world of Robotics in Hawaii.

2011 APEC Exhibition

Held from November 7th through November 13th 2011 and located in the Hawaii Convention Center, TKM volunteered to man a booth for ROC Hawaii. The team was visited by other exhibitors, volunteers, and delegates who asked about ROC's mission and its role in Robotics on the islands. People who stopped by were also able to try out VEX robots to really understand the program. The team represented teams from all over the island at APEC, and remained at the ROC booth for the duration of the nearly week-long event.

On November 20th, 2011, the Robotics team volunteered to demo their 2010 FRC robot and their 2011 VEX Gateway Robots at the Palama Settlement's "Malama Palama: Festival of 100 Voices" event. The event was held to mark the hundredth year anniversary since the settlement's establishment.

Tiger Showcase Night

Tiger Showcase is an annual event at McKinley High School aimed at helping prospective underclassmen to choose their courses for the next year. At the event, Team 368 showed off their 2011 FRC robot and their two most successful 2011 VEX Gateway robots and answered questions from curious parents and students. The showcase lasted four hours, with members explaining Robotics and its impacts on both the social and academic aspects of life.

McKinley Mini Math Meet

On February 4th, 2012, Team Kika Mana gave a presentation and brief demo to the attendees of the McKinley High School Mini Math Meet, an event hosted by the McKinley Math Team for math teams from a few of Hawaii's middle schools. The presentation explained the VEX and FIRST Robotics programs, as well as the team's activities, followed by an explanation of the 2010 Breakaway robot.

2012 Honolulu Festival

The McKinley Robotics Team volunteered at the Honolulu Festival (March 3rd-4th, 2012) alongside the Cool Geeks from Moanalua High School (Team #2348). Together, the teams held demonstrations of VEX Gateway robots and our very own 2010 FRC robot while handing out flyers and answering questions about the Robotics programs. The demo was also interactive, with children and adults getting behind the controls of the VEX bots and shooting soccer balls back and forth between the FRC bot.

Ka'ahumanu Elementary FLL Showcase

Queen Ka'ahumanu Elementary School invited Team Kika Mana to be guest speakers at their FLL Showcase on March 8th, 2012. The showcase included a demonstration from the elementary school's FIRST LEGO League team as well as from our very own VEX and FRC teams. The demonstrations were followed by an interactive exercise orchestrated by FLL team members; parents and students got to race each other to build the tallest LEGO tower in a short amount of time. An explanation of the FIRST program was given by our members with emphasis on the mentor-student experiences and relationships forged through hard work. People attending the showcase were able to see our 2010 FRC robot in action in the school cafeteria and also on screen as footage from our past matches were played.
