Students

Why should I participate in FIRST Robotics?
FIRST can be supplementary to the education you are already receiving at school; by joining FIRST, you can apply skills you learn in your classes through real-world practices by building a robot. In addition, you are given various opportunities to think outside the box and exercise your sense of creativity.

Can any student find a role on a FIRST team?
Definitely! FIRST is not only for people who enjoy designing and building robots; other crucial parts of the team require students who are able to create computer graphics, handle the team's public relations, photograph at competitions and during the season, document progress, and even help organize fundraisers.

Is there any money involved?
Students and mentors do not get paid for building robots and competing. However, there are opportunities to obtain college scholarships; over $12 million in scholarships from over 133 sources are available to student participants in FIRST. Click here for more information.

Is this part of school or is it an extracurricular?
This all depends on which school you attend, but there is a high probability that FIRST is an extracurricular.

What kind of things can I learn from FIRST?
FIRST provides students a wide range of information and skills to be learned. Technical skills, such as programming, electronics, welding, and machining, interpersonal communication skills such as website design or public speaking, and even finance-related skills, such as budget management. FIRST also promotes the growth of leadership qualities and cooperation, as well as Gracious Professionalism.

What other benefits might there be for joining?
Robotics teams are not just groups of people looking for personal gain; teams are families, with bonds that were made through hard work, long hours, and common interests. Robotics is a place where you can meet new people and get to know them on a deeper level than you would if you had met them in another club or class. These people are not limited only to your school or the state, you are also given various opportunities to become acquainted with students from other states and countries.


Parents

My child is interested in robotics, but their school does not have a FIRST Robotics program. What should I do?
­The best thing to do is to talk to the principal of your child's school, as he or she would have the authority to make things happen. Not all teams are school teams, though; several teams are formed from organizations such as the Girl Scouts or home schools, which may be willing to take your child in.

How will FIRST help my child after high school?
FIRST opens up many opportunities for students after high school. Dozens of colleges offer scholarships for students who have participated on a FIRST Robotics team. Sometimes, these can lead to mentorship and career opportunities as well!

How can I help a FIRST Robotics Team?
Parental involvement is always encouraged; ask the team advisor if he or she needs help with planning, chaperoning, etc.

What other robotics competitions are there besides FRC?
FIRST hosts 3 other robotics competitions, FIRST LEGO League (FLL), Junior FIRST LEGO League (JFLL), and FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC). The LEGO competitions are for elementary to middle school students while the Tech Challenge is a low-cost alternative for high school students.

KIPR (Kiss Institiute for Practical Robotics) hosts Botball, a LEGO based competition for middle and high school students.

MATE (Marine Advanced Technology Education) hosts a very unique type of robotics competition. These robots operate underwater and are controlled by human operators above the water.


Educators

Why start a FIRST team?
By starting a FIRST team, a high school can change the lives of hundreds of students. FIRST Robotics competitions can get students excited about science and technology and inspire them to pursue careers in those fields. Add to that the relationships FIRST team members establish with their mentors as well as the challenge and fun of building a robot, and you have an effective formula for learning that is unlike any other.

Who do I talk to if I am interest in starting a FIRST Robotics team at my school?
You will most likely need the support of the school administration and the school Principal. Talk to the Principal about what FIRST is and direct him or her to the FIRST Website for more information. If you aren't knowledgeable about electronics, programming, and building things, you should also find someone who is.

How many teachers should help with a FRC (FIRST Robotics Competition) team?
Only one teacher is required to be the team's advisor, but try to get as many teachers involved as possible.

How much does it cost to enter a FRC competition?
The entry fee for an initial regional if you competed in the previous year is $5000. For those who did not compete in the previous year, it is $5500. At some regionals, rookie teams can apply for a grant to cover this expense. Each additional regional is $4000 and participation in the World Championship is $5000.
